# Lesson 09 — The Mood Board View

**Duration:** 8 minutes
**Outcome:** You generate a mood board for any vibe and use it to drive your next prompt.
**Prerequisites:** Lesson 04 complete.

---

## Context (for the LMS module page)

Sometimes you can feel a vibe but you cannot describe it. The Mood Board View bridges that gap. You give the app a feeling, a setting, or a color and it generates a visual mood board you can read at a glance. Use it as creative direction before you write a single prompt. This is the view that turns abstract feelings into specific musical decisions.

## What You Will Learn
- When a mood board beats a written brief
- How to generate a mood board from a feeling, setting, or color
- How to read the visual cues
- How to translate the board into a Songwriting or Instrumental prompt

---

## Step-by-Step Guide

1. Open any mode and click the MOOD BOARD view tab.
2. Type a feeling, setting, or color. Example: "Late summer night driving through the city alone, neon reflections on wet pavement, longing but free."
3. Hit generate.
4. Read the visual board the app produces.
5. Note the recurring tones, textures, and energy cues.
6. Switch to Chat View (in Songwriting or Instrumental Mode).
7. Reference the mood board in your prompt: "Use the mood board I just generated as the creative direction."
8. Generate the song or beat.

## Screen Recording Script

### [00:00 — 00:30] Cold open

**[ON SCREEN]** Mood Board View active.

**Narration:**
"Mood Board View. When you can feel the vibe but the words are not coming, this is where you start. Eight minutes."

### [00:30 — 01:30] When to use it

**[ON SCREEN]** Face cam.

**Narration:**
"Use the Mood Board when you have a feeling but you cannot describe it. Maybe a friend sent you a photo and said make me a song that sounds like this. Maybe you woke up with a color in your head. The Mood Board lets you start from a vibe instead of a vocabulary."

### [01:30 — 03:30] Generating a board

**[ON SCREEN]** Type: "Late summer night driving through the city alone, neon reflections on wet pavement, longing but free." Hit generate.

**Narration:**
"Watch. I am going to type a feeling. Late summer night driving through the city alone, neon reflections on wet pavement, longing but free. Generate. The board comes back. I get a visual collage of tones, textures, and energy cues that match the description."

### [03:30 — 05:00] Reading the board

**[ON SCREEN]** Slow scroll through the generated board. Point at each element.

**Narration:**
"Read it like a producer reads a reference track. What colors are dominant. What textures are repeating. Is the energy steady or does it pulse. The app uses this same board to inform any prompt you write next, so the more you understand it, the better the prompt becomes."

### [05:00 — 07:00] Translating to a prompt

**[ON SCREEN]** Switch to Chat View in Songwriting Mode. Type: "Use the mood board I just generated as the creative direction. Late-night R&B with dreamy guitars and laid back drums."

**Narration:**
"Switch to Chat View. Type your prompt and reference the mood board. The app pulls the visual cues into the prompt automatically. Watch. Send. The Style Prompt that comes back uses warm pads, distant guitars, and a laid back groove. Same vibe as the board, translated into music."

### [07:00 — 07:45] When not to use it

**[ON SCREEN]** Face cam.

**Narration:**
"Quick note. You do not need a mood board for every session. If you already know what you want, just write the prompt. Mood boards are for the moments when the vibe is real but the words are not there yet."

### [07:45 — 08:00] Close

**Narration:**
"Next lesson, DNA Profiles. This is where the app starts learning your sound. See you there."
