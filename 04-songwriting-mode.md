# Lesson 04 — Songwriting Mode

**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Outcome:** You generate a full Style Prompt and Lyric Prompt, paste them into Suno, and get a track back.
**Prerequisites:** Logged in. Suno account open in another tab.

---

## Context (for the LMS module page)

Songwriting Mode is the heart of Suno Writer. You give it a feeling, a topic, or a vibe and it gives you back two separate prompts: a Style Prompt that controls the sound, and a Lyric Prompt that controls the words and structure. Both are formatted exactly how Suno wants them, with no clichés, no artist names, and the song structure that actually works for radio-length tracks. This is the lesson where the app stops being theoretical and starts making real music.

## What You Will Learn
- When to use Songwriting Mode
- The two outputs and why they live in separate copy blocks
- How to upload a reference track for tempo and key matching
- The Complexity knob in practice (0, 50, 100)
- The Explicit and Clean rating toggle
- The Myind Sound song structure
- The cliché blacklist and why we enforce it
- How to paste both prompts into Suno and generate

---

## Step-by-Step Guide

1. Open Suno Writer and click Songwriting Mode.
2. Confirm you are in the CHAT view tab.
3. (Optional) Upload a reference track using the upload button. The app will use it as a silent reference for tempo and key.
4. Set the Complexity knob to 50 for your first run.
5. Set the Rating toggle to Explicit or Clean depending on your release.
6. Type your idea in plain English. Example: "Late-night studio R&B song about staying focused while everyone else is sleeping."
7. Hit send and wait for the response.
8. The app returns two code blocks. The first is labeled 🎵 STYLE PROMPT (label is OUTSIDE the copy box). The second is labeled 📝 LYRICS PROMPT.
9. Click the copy icon on the Style Prompt. Paste it into the Suno Style field.
10. Click the copy icon on the Lyrics Prompt. Paste it into the Suno Lyrics field.
11. Generate the song in Suno.
12. Listen back. If you want changes, type a follow-up in the chat ("make the bridge darker, add a beat switch on the second hook"). The app remembers context.
13. When you love it, click Save in the sidebar. Pick a name and tag it.

## Screen Recording Script

### [00:00 — 00:30] Cold open

**[ON SCREEN]** Songwriting Mode active, empty chat input.

**Narration:**
"Songwriting Mode. This is where you turn an idea into a finished Suno prompt in under a minute. By the end of these 15 minutes you will have a full song generated and playing back."

### [00:30 — 01:30] When to use it

**[ON SCREEN]** Face cam.

**Narration:**
"Use Songwriting Mode when you want a full song with lyrics. If you want a beat with no vocals, that is Instrumental Mode in the next lesson. If you want a drumless loop to chop, that is Sample Maker. But if the words and the song structure matter, this is your mode."

### [01:30 — 02:30] The two outputs

**[ON SCREEN]** A previous example output visible. Highlight both code blocks and the labels above them.

**Narration:**
"Every response gives you two separate code blocks. One Style Prompt for the sound. One Lyrics Prompt for the words and structure. Notice the labels live OUTSIDE the copy box. That is intentional. When you click copy you only get the prompt, not the label. Suno does not need the label."

### [02:30 — 04:00] Reference track upload (optional)

**[ON SCREEN]** Click upload button, drag in an audio file, watch it process.

**Narration:**
"If you have a reference track, drag it in here. The app uses it as a silent reference for tempo and key. It is not going to copy it. It is going to use it as creative direction. This is how you keep your sessions cohesive across an EP. I drop the lead single in here and every other track on the project stays in the same sonic family."

### [04:00 — 05:00] The Complexity knob

**[ON SCREEN]** Slowly turn the Complexity knob from 0 to 50 to 100.

**Narration:**
"Complexity knob. Zero is sparse. Think one melody, one chord pattern, simple. Fifty is standard. This is where most of your work lives. One hundred is dense with key changes, beat switches, dropouts, and detailed per-section cues. Start at fifty. Push it higher when the song needs to feel like an event."

### [05:00 — 05:30] Explicit and Clean

**[ON SCREEN]** Click between Explicit and Clean toggle.

**Narration:**
"Rating toggle. Explicit means the lyrics will reflect real life with real language. Clean means radio-friendly. Same energy, different word choices. Pick based on where the song is going to live."

### [05:30 — 06:30] The Myind Sound song structure

**[ON SCREEN]** Pull up the Song Structure card overlay.

**Narration:**
"Every song this app writes uses the same structure. Intro, hook, verse one, hook, verse two, bridge, hook, outro. Not the academic intro verse pre-chorus chorus thing. This structure is built for the way modern songs actually land. The hook hits early. The bridge always modulates with a key change. The intro is always instrumental unless you ask for vocals."

### [06:30 — 07:30] The cliché blacklist

**[ON SCREEN]** Pull up the Cliché Blacklist card overlay.

**Narration:**
"And the app enforces a blacklist. You will never see in my chest, pressure makes me fold, flame, light, storm, scars, or manifest in your output. These are the words that make AI lyrics sound like AI lyrics. We strip them out so your songs sound like a human wrote them."

### [07:30 — 11:00] Live build

**[ON SCREEN]** Type a real prompt: "Late-night studio R&B song about staying focused while everyone else is sleeping." Hit send. Wait for response. Highlight Style Prompt copy box. Highlight Lyric Prompt copy box.

**Narration:**
"Let's actually build one. I am going to type, late-night studio R&B song about staying focused while everyone else is sleeping. Send. Watch the response come back. Style Prompt up top. Lyric Prompt below. Let me read you what it gave me."

[Read the actual generated output briefly. Point out the absence of clichés. Point out the song structure tags.]

### [11:00 — 13:30] Pasting into Suno

**[ON SCREEN]** Open Suno in another tab. Paste Style Prompt into the Style field. Paste Lyric Prompt into the Lyrics field. Hit Create.

**Narration:**
"Now I am going to copy the Style Prompt, switch to Suno, paste it in the Style field. Switch back. Copy the Lyric Prompt. Paste it in the Lyrics field. Hit Create. While that generates, I want you to notice what just happened. Two clicks. Two pastes. That is the entire workflow. No guessing."

### [13:30 — 14:30] Iterating

**[ON SCREEN]** Suno result plays back briefly. Switch back to Suno Writer chat. Type a follow-up: "Make the bridge darker, add a beat switch on the second hook."

**Narration:**
"If you want changes, do not start over. Type a follow-up in the chat. The app remembers everything. Make the bridge darker. Add a beat switch on the second hook. Send. New prompts come back. Re-paste into Suno. This is how you iterate fast."

### [14:30 — 15:00] Save and close

**[ON SCREEN]** Click Save in sidebar, name the work, tag it.

**Narration:**
"When you love it, click Save in the sidebar. Name it. Tag it. It is now in your Saved Works library forever. Homework: build one full song and post it in the community with hashtag sunowriter lesson four. Next lesson, we drop the vocals and build a beat."
