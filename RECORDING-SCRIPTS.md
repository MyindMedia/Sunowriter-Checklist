# Suno Writer — Recording Scripts & Talking Points

Loose, conversational scripts for all 15 lessons. Not broadcast copy. These are talking points you read once, internalize, and riff from on camera. Keep it peer to peer. No em dashes. No buzzwords. Talk to one person, not an audience.

---

## The Lesson Arc (why the order matters)

Think of Module 01 as one long story told in three acts.

**Act 1 — Orientation (Lessons 1, 2, 3).** You show them what Suno Writer is, walk them around the app, and get them logged in. At the end of Act 1 they should feel "OK, I know where everything lives, I know why this exists."

**Act 2 — The Four Modes (Lessons 4, 5, 6, 7).** One mode per lesson, each standing alone. Songwriting first because it is the headline use case. Instrumental second because it is the second most common. Sample Maker third because it is the creative wildcard. Sample Analysis fourth because it is the "aha" moment that sells the whole app.

**Act 3 — Power Features & Workflow (Lessons 8 through 15).** Once they know every mode they are ready for the deeper features: Arrange, Mood Board, DNA Profiles, Saved Works, Release Prep, then the full end to end workflow, troubleshooting, and a recap with homework. This is where you turn them from "I can use the app" into "I have a system."

**Recording tip:** Record Lessons 1 and 15 last. You will be way more confident about what to say in the intro and outro after you have recorded the middle, and the intro is the first thing new members see so it needs to be tight.

---

## Voice Rules (apply to every lesson)

- Talk like you are in a DM with one person
- Use "you" a lot, never "the user" or "students"
- Name the struggle before you name the feature ("if you have ever..." hooks)
- Own your authority without bragging. "I have been doing this for X years" is fine. "As an industry expert..." is not
- Laugh if something is funny. If you mess up a take, keep going and edit it out in post
- Every lesson ends with a clear handoff to the next one
- No em dashes, no "in this video we are going to"

## LESSON 01 — Welcome to Suno Writer (5 min)

**Vibe:** Warm cold open. Establish why you built this. No features yet.

**Cold open hook (pick one):**
- "If you have ever typed a prompt into Suno and got back something that sounds nothing like what you heard in your head, this whole module is for you."
- "Raise your hand if you have wasted credits on Suno outputs that did not sound anything like you."

**Talking points:**
1. What it is in one sentence — "Suno Writer is the tool I built so you never have to fight the AI again."
2. Who you are — quick. "I am Lawrence, I run Myind Sound Music Club, I have been making beats and releasing music for years."
3. The origin — "I had a custom GPT doing this for me privately for over a year. The community kept asking for access. So I rebuilt it as a real app."
4. Who this is for — "Beatmakers fleshing out full songs. Songwriters testing ideas fast. Producers who need to translate a vague vibe into a real direction."
5. What they will walk away with — "By the end of this module you will use Suno like a professional tool, not a slot machine."
6. Preview the 4 modes — say each name once. Do not explain them yet.

**Close:** "In the next lesson I am going to walk you around the interface so you know exactly where everything lives. Hit next, I will see you over there."

**Don't:**
- Don't explain features. Save it.
- Don't apologize ("I hope you like it"). Just deliver.

## LESSON 02 — The Interface Tour (6 min)

**Vibe:** Physical tour. You are showing a friend around your studio.

**Cold open:** "Before we touch a single feature let me just show you where everything is. Thirty seconds of orientation now saves you thirty minutes of confusion later."

**Talking points:**
1. The header: mode switcher, view switcher (Chat / Arrange / Mood Board), profile. "This is the control tower. You are almost always switching between modes up here."
2. The main chat panel in the middle. "This is where the conversation happens. You talk to it, it talks back, you go back and forth until the output is right."
3. The H-Pads on the right side. "Rhythm Builder, Harmonic Shift, Texture and Depth. We will play with these for real later. For now just know they are your shape-the-sound dials."
4. The Complexity knob. "One dial. Left for simple, right for dense. I live around 60 percent."
5. The sidebar: Saved Works, Templates, DNA Profiles. "This is your stash. Anything you love you can save and come back to."
6. The footer bar. "Usage, status, credits. Glance at it. Move on."

**Close:** "You have the map. Next lesson we actually log in and make sure your account is set up right."

**Don't:**
- Don't go deep on any single feature. This is the overview.
- Don't skip the H-Pads just because they feel weird to explain. Name them, promise you will come back to them.

## LESSON 03 — First Login (4 min)

**Vibe:** Practical, short. You are standing next to them clicking through.

**Cold open:** "Quick one. Let me get you logged in and set up so the rest of this module actually works for you."

**Talking points:**
1. Go to the app URL. Show the exact URL on screen.
2. Sign in with whichever provider you want. Walk through it once.
3. First time in, confirm your community handle so your saves sync.
4. Set a default mode if you have a strong preference. "I default to Songwriting Mode because that is what I use most."
5. Bookmark the tab. Seriously, do it now. "Future you will thank present you."

**Close:** "You are in. Now the fun starts. Next lesson we go deep on Songwriting Mode."

**Don't:**
- Don't let this go past 5 minutes. It is a setup lesson, not a feature lesson.

## LESSON 04 — Songwriting Mode (10 min)

**Vibe:** This is the main event. Energy up. Show off what it can do.

**Cold open:** "This is the lesson most of you are here for. Songwriting Mode. By the end of this ten minutes you will have a full Suno prompt for a song you can actually release."

**Talking points:**
1. What Songwriting Mode does. "Takes your idea, asks the right questions, gives you back a Style Prompt and a Lyric Prompt, both ready to paste into Suno."
2. Start with a prompt. Something specific. "Let me show you with a real example. I am going to ask it for a moody late-night R&B record about missing someone you should not miss."
3. Watch it ask clarifying questions. "Notice it does not just dump a generic response. It wants to know the tempo, the era, the vocal vibe. That is the difference between this and a generic chatbot."
4. The output comes back in two clean blocks. "Style Prompt on top, Lyric Prompt underneath. Labels are outside the code blocks so you can copy them separately." Point at it.
5. The structure. "Intro, Hook, Verse, Hook, Verse, Bridge, Hook, Outro. Every time. Bridge modulates. Intro is instrumental. These are the rules I release on and the app enforces them."
6. Length target. "Two thirty to three thirty. Radio friendly without feeling like an edit."
7. Copy both prompts, paste into Suno, hit generate. Show the actual Suno output if you can.
8. React to it on camera. Honest reaction. "OK the verse cadence is exactly what I wanted. The hook could be punchier, let me go back and tweak."
9. Show the back and forth. "You are not stuck with the first output. Talk to it. Tell it what to change."

**Close:** "That is Songwriting Mode. Next up, Instrumental Mode for when you do not need lyrics at all."

**Don't:**
- Don't rush this one. It is the headline feature.
- Don't use placeholder examples. Make a real song idea.

## LESSON 05 — Instrumental Mode (8 min)

**Vibe:** For beatmakers and remixers. Different energy from Songwriting.

**Cold open:** "Not every idea needs lyrics. If you are making beats, building instrumental hooks, or remixing something, this mode is your home."

**Talking points:**
1. Switch to Instrumental Mode. Point at the header.
2. What is different. "No Lyric Prompt. Just the Style Prompt, but way more detailed on the sonic side, because there are no vocals carrying the weight."
3. Example. "Let me pull up a beat pack I am working on. I want a dark trap instrumental with horror-movie strings and an 808 that rattles."
4. Show the prompt flow. "It is going to ask about BPM, key, arrangement. Be specific. Garbage in, garbage out."
5. The output. "Look at how much instrument detail is in there. This is the level Suno actually needs to deliver."
6. Copy, paste, generate. Show the Suno result.
7. Quick callout on remixing. "Same mode. Describe the original in sonic terms, describe what you want to change, go."

**Close:** "That is instrumentals. Next lesson we get into Sample Maker Mode, which is my favorite to play with."

**Don't:**
- Don't overlap with the Songwriting lesson. Assume they watched it.

## LESSON 06 — Sample Maker Mode (7 min)

**Vibe:** Playful. This mode is fun and it should sound fun.

**Cold open:** "You know how producers used to dig through dusty vinyl looking for a four-bar loop to flip? Sample Maker Mode is that, except the record store is infinite and it is waiting for you."

**Talking points:**
1. What it is. "You describe the vibe of a sample you wish existed and the app writes a Suno prompt that generates a loop you can chop."
2. Drumless by default. "Critical. You do not want drums in your sample source because you are layering your own."
3. Example. "I want a dusty 70s soul sample. Rhodes, strings, a warm tape hiss, no drums, in B flat minor, around 80 BPM."
4. Show the prompt. "Notice it specifies the era, the instruments, and the imperfections. That is what gives it character."
5. Paste into Suno, generate, bring it into your DAW. "I am going to load this into Ableton and chop it live for you."
6. Show the chop. "Two bars pitched up, two bars time-stretched, filter on the top end. That is a beat now."
7. The licensing callout. "These are your samples. You made them. Nothing to clear."

**Close:** "Sample Maker Mode. Use it. Next lesson is the one that blew my mind the first time I tested it. Sample Analysis."

**Don't:**
- Don't skip the DAW chop demo. It sells the mode.

## LESSON 07 — Sample Analysis Mode (8 min)

**Vibe:** "Watch this" energy. This is the demo moment.

**Cold open:** "OK, this one is going to look like magic. Stick with me. You upload any track you love and the app tells you exactly what makes it sound the way it sounds, then gives you a Suno prompt to recreate the vibe."

**Talking points:**
1. Switch to Sample Analysis Mode.
2. Upload a reference track. Pick something interesting. "I am uploading a section of a record I have been obsessed with this week."
3. Watch it work. "Under the hood it is doing a six-layer audio analysis. Tempo, key, rhythm, harmony, texture, density. I will not bore you with the math. What matters is what comes out."
4. The breakdown. "Look at this. It tells you the BPM, the key, the time signature, the instrumentation, the mixing style, even the emotional vibe in plain English."
5. The generated prompt. "And then here is the Suno prompt. Style Prompt and Lyric Prompt, ready to paste. This is the prompt that would have recreated the vibe of the uploaded track."
6. Run it in Suno. React to the result. "Not a copy. A cousin. Same DNA, different family."
7. The creative use. "This is how you reverse engineer any sound you love. Not to steal it. To understand the recipe and cook your own version."

**Close:** "That is the four modes. You now know what every mode does and when to reach for it. Next lesson we go up a level and start using Arrange View to see your song as a song."

**Don't:**
- Don't use a copyrighted track you cannot show. Pick something safe or use your own.
- Don't undersell this. It is the most impressive feature.

## LESSON 08 — Arrange View (6 min)

**Vibe:** "Let me show you a different way to see this."

**Cold open:** "Chat View is great for conversation. But sometimes you need to see your whole song laid out like a blueprint. That is Arrange View."

**Talking points:**
1. Switch to Arrange View. Point at the toggle.
2. What you are looking at. "Every section of your song as a block. Intro, Hook, Verse, Hook. You can see the whole structure at a glance."
3. Rearrange by dragging. "Grab a block, drop it. The prompt updates automatically."
4. Edit a single section. "Click into any block and change just that section without touching the rest."
5. Lock sections. "Love your hook? Lock it. Regenerate everything else. The hook stays."
6. When to use Arrange View versus Chat View. "Chat for exploring. Arrange for committing."

**Close:** "That is Arrange. Next up, Mood Board View, which is where we get visual."

## LESSON 09 — Mood Board View (5 min)

**Vibe:** Visual, creative. Slower pace.

**Cold open:** "Some ideas are not words. They are a feeling, a color, a reference track, a photo from a road trip. Mood Board View is where all of that lives."

**Talking points:**
1. Switch to Mood Board View.
2. Drop references. "Images, audio clips, text fragments. Anything that captures the vibe."
3. How the app reads them. "It uses the board as context when you start writing prompts. So your output actually reflects what you pinned."
4. Example. "I am building a summer EP. I dropped in four photos, two audio references, and a color palette. Watch what the prompt becomes now."
5. The creative unlock. "This is how you keep an album consistent. One board, one vibe, every song feeds from it."

**Close:** "Arrange and Mood Board together are how you go from one song to a cohesive project. Next lesson, DNA Profiles, which is where this gets personal."

## LESSON 10 — DNA Profiles (8 min)

**Vibe:** "I am going to show you something you will probably use every day."

**Cold open:** "Every artist has a sound. A signature. DNA Profiles is how you teach Suno Writer yours so it stops giving you generic output and starts sounding like you."

**Talking points:**
1. What a DNA Profile is. "A saved sonic fingerprint. Your tempo range, your key preferences, your go-to instruments, your vocal vibe, your lyrical themes. All in one profile."
2. Build one live. "Let me make my DNA Profile from scratch so you see it happen."
3. Save it. Name it.
4. Match. "Now I am going to upload a track that is not mine and have the app tell me how close it matches my DNA."
5. Blend. "And now the magic move. I am going to blend my DNA with a reference artist's style. Output is somewhere in the middle. That is how you grow without losing yourself."
6. Switch profiles. "You can have a DNA Profile for your solo work, another for your production work, another for a side project. Switch them like hats."

**Close:** "DNA Profiles are the difference between using the tool and owning the tool. Next lesson, Saved Works and Templates, which is where we stop losing good ideas."

## LESSON 11 — Saved Works, Templates, Collab (6 min)

**Vibe:** Organized, practical. "Here is how you stop losing good ideas."

**Cold open:** "Be honest. How many good ideas have you lost because you forgot where you saved them? This lesson fixes that forever."

**Talking points:**
1. Saved Works. "Every prompt, every output, every variation. One place."
2. How to save. Tag it, name it.
3. Search and filter. "By date, by mode, by DNA profile. Find anything in seconds."
4. Templates. "If you keep writing similar prompts, save the skeleton as a template. Load it next time, tweak the details, done."
5. Collab mode. "Share a Saved Work with someone else in the community. They can remix your prompt without overwriting your original."
6. My actual workflow. "I have about forty templates. I never start from blank anymore."

**Close:** "You have saves, templates, and collab. Next lesson, Release Prep, which is how we get from prompt to a track that is actually ready to post."

## LESSON 12 — Release Prep & Clean Lyrics (7 min)

**Vibe:** "Time to ship." End-of-process energy.

**Cold open:** "You have the song. It sounds good. But is it actually ready to release? This lesson is the finish line check."

**Talking points:**
1. The Release Prep checklist. "Title locked in. Explicit flag set. Metadata filled. Cover art queued. The app walks you through all of it."
2. Clean Lyrics generator. "If your track has explicit content and you need a radio edit, one click and it gives you a clean version that still scans."
3. Why this matters. "Spotify, Apple Music, TikTok. Every platform has different rules. The app catches stuff you will forget."
4. Export. "One click, everything you need for distribution in one package."

**Close:** "Your track is release-ready. Next lesson is the big one. The full end to end workflow from blank page to finished song."

## LESSON 13 — End to End Workflow (12 min)

**Vibe:** The capstone. Longer, slower, let it breathe.

**Cold open:** "We have covered every mode and every feature. Now let me show you how I actually use all of this to make a song from nothing. Zero to finished in real time."

**Talking points:**
1. Start with a seed. "I am going to pick one idea right now on camera. Whatever comes to mind. No prep."
2. Open the right mode. Explain why.
3. Pull up my DNA profile. "This is step one for me, always."
4. Write the first prompt. Watch the conversation happen.
5. Generate in Suno. First output.
6. React, tweak, regenerate. "This is the part nobody shows you. The second, third, fifth pass."
7. Switch to Arrange View and lock the hook.
8. Use Sample Analysis to pull a vibe from a reference.
9. Final prompt goes to Suno. Final output.
10. Run Release Prep. Export.
11. Timestamp the whole thing. "That took me X minutes start to finish."

**Close:** "That is the whole workflow. Next lesson we hit the common mistakes and tips I wish someone had told me on day one."

**Don't:**
- Don't edit out the tweaking and regenerating. That is the most valuable part of the lesson.

## LESSON 14 — Tips & Troubleshooting (6 min)

**Vibe:** Casual, listy. "Here is what I have learned."

**Cold open:** "Quick lesson. No features. Just the stuff that would have saved me hours when I started."

**Talking points:**
1. If your output sounds generic, your prompt is too vague. "Double the detail. Triple it."
2. No artist names in prompts. "Use sonic descriptors. 'Breathy soulful female vocal' beats 'sounds like Jorja Smith' every time."
3. Never trust the first output. "Second pass is usually where it gets good."
4. Save everything. "Even the takes you hate. You will reuse them later."
5. The cliché blacklist. "If you see 'hustle,' 'grind,' 'straight from the bottom' in your lyrics, throw it out. The app flags them but you have to pay attention."
6. Credit management. "Use Suno's cheapest model for drafts. Upgrade only when you are dialing in the final."
7. If you get stuck, ask the app to explain what it just did. "It will teach you its own logic."

**Close:** "Tips in your back pocket. One more lesson and you are done."

## LESSON 15 — Recap & Challenge (5 min)

**Vibe:** Proud, warm, motivating. Send them off with homework.

**Cold open:** "You made it to the end of Module 01. Take a second. You know this tool better than ninety percent of people using Suno right now."

**Talking points:**
1. Recap the arc. "Four modes, three views, DNA profiles, the full workflow."
2. What they can do now. "You can take any idea, in any genre, and turn it into a release-ready Suno prompt in under thirty minutes."
3. The challenge. "Homework. Make one song. Any mode. Any genre. Post it in the community channel with the tag #SunoWriterModule1."
4. What you want to see. "I am going to personally listen to every one that gets posted. Best three get a feature on the Myind Sound page."
5. What is next. "Module 02 is about to go live. We are going deeper on release strategy, DNA profile blending, and building a full EP with the app."
6. Close warm. "I am proud of you for finishing this. Now go make something. I will see you in the community."

**Don't:**
- Don't make the homework optional. People need the nudge.
- Don't promise a Module 02 date unless you have one locked.

## Recording Order (not lesson order)

Record in this order, not the order they appear in the module:

1. Lessons 4, 5, 6, 7 (the four modes — build confidence)
2. Lessons 8, 9 (Arrange, Mood Board — quick wins)
3. Lessons 10, 11, 12 (DNA, Saves, Release Prep — systems)
4. Lesson 13 (end to end — you are warmed up now)
5. Lessons 2, 3 (interface tour, first login — short, easy)
6. Lesson 14 (tips — you know the gotchas by now)
7. Lessons 1, 15 (intro and outro — record LAST, when you know exactly what tone to set)

By the time you get to Lesson 1, you will be so confident about the module that the intro will write itself.
