Lesson 07 — Sample Analysis Mode

Duration: 12 minutes Outcome: You upload any audio file, get a full breakdown, and walk away with a Suno prompt that recreates the vibe. Prerequisites: Lessons 04 to 06 complete.

Context (for the LMS module page)

Sample Analysis Mode is the most technically powerful feature in Suno Writer. You upload any track and the app runs a six-layer Librosa analysis (key, BPM, chord progression, energy map, tonal character, groove, structure) and then layers Gemini's qualitative listening on top. The output is a full sonic breakdown plus a ready-to-paste Suno prompt that recreates the vibe. Artist names get auto-scrubbed to sonic descriptions, which protects you legally and forces you to think in sound, not labels.

What You Will Learn

  • What gets analyzed and how
  • The six-layer Librosa pipeline in plain English
  • How Gemini's qualitative listening adds context
  • How to use the analysis to recreate vs reference a vibe
  • Why artist names get scrubbed and why that protects you
  • How to combine Sample Analysis with Songwriting or Instrumental Mode

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Click Sample Analysis Mode.
  2. Click the upload button or drag an audio file directly into the chat area.
  3. Wait for the analysis to complete (usually under 30 seconds).
  4. Read the breakdown: key, BPM, chord progression, energy curve, tonal character, groove, structure.
  5. Scroll to the generated Style Prompt and Lyric Prompt at the bottom.
  6. Notice that any artist references in the analysis are translated to sonic descriptions instead of artist names.
  7. Copy both prompts into Suno to recreate the vibe.
  8. (Bonus) Switch back to Songwriting Mode and use the analysis as a reference for a brand new song in the same sonic family.
