Lesson 06 — Sample Maker Mode

Duration: 10 minutes Outcome: You generate a drumless, chop-ready loop you can drag into your DAW and sample. Prerequisites: Lessons 04 and 05 complete.

Context (for the LMS module page)

Sample Maker Mode is the secret weapon. It generates drumless songs and loops designed to be chopped, flipped, and sampled the way producers have been sampling soul, jazz, and orchestral records for decades. No drums, ever. The hook always comes first so you can chop it immediately. Use it to create your own sample library that nobody else has access to.

What You Will Learn

  • Why drumless and chop-ready matters
  • The genres this mode is built for
  • The "no drums, organic percussion only" rule
  • Why the hook comes first
  • How to feed the output back into Instrumental Mode for a full beat

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Click Sample Maker Mode.
  2. Type your idea. Example: "Motown soul loop, female vocal lead, warm tape compression, hopeful and bittersweet."
  3. Hit send.
  4. Read the Style Prompt. Notice it specifies no drums and only organic percussion (tambourine, congas, shakers).
  5. Read the Lyric Prompt. The hook always appears first.
  6. Copy both into Suno and generate.
  7. Download the Suno result.
  8. Drag the file into your DAW.
  9. Chop the hook section first.
  10. (Bonus) Take the same vibe back to Instrumental Mode and generate a beat that sits underneath your chop.
