# Lesson 11 — Saved Works, Templates & Collaboration

**Duration:** 10 minutes
**Outcome:** You manage your library, fork a community template, and share a session with another member.
**Prerequisites:** Lessons 04 to 10 complete.

---

## Context (for the LMS module page)

Suno Writer is a community tool, not just a personal one. Saved Works keeps everything you have ever generated organized and searchable. Templates is a community library where members share their best prompts and you can fork any of them as your starting point. Conversations let you share an entire session with a collaborator, complete with permissions. This lesson is how you stop working alone and start working with the whole Myind Sound community.

## What You Will Learn
- How Saved Works are organized and filtered
- How to favorite and version a saved work
- How to browse and fork community templates
- How to submit your own template for community approval
- How to share a conversation with another member
- Collaboration etiquette inside the community

---

## Step-by-Step Guide

1. Open the sidebar and click Saved Works.
2. Use the mode filter to show only one mode at a time.
3. Click any saved work to reopen the conversation in full.
4. Click the star icon to favorite a work.
5. Click the version history icon to see all previous versions of the same prompt.
6. Open the sidebar and click Templates.
7. Filter templates by mode or genre.
8. Find one you like and click Fork. The template loads in your active mode as a starting point.
9. To submit your own, click Submit Template, fill in the form, and send for admin approval.
10. Open Conversations from the sidebar.
11. Click any conversation and click Share. Enter a collaborator email.
12. The collaborator gets access. You can revoke any time from the Collaborators panel.

## Screen Recording Script

### [00:00 — 00:30] Cold open

**[ON SCREEN]** Saved Works panel open.

**Narration:**
"Saved Works, Templates, and collaboration. This lesson turns Suno Writer from a personal tool into a community engine. Ten minutes."

### [00:30 — 02:30] Saved Works tour

**[ON SCREEN]** Open Saved Works. Filter by mode. Open a work. Favorite it. Show version history.

**Narration:**
"Saved Works lives in the sidebar. Every prompt you save lands here. Use the mode filter to show only Songwriting, only Instrumental, or all modes. Click any saved work to reopen the full conversation. Click the star to favorite the ones you come back to most. And the version history icon shows you every version of the same prompt over time, so you can compare how a song evolved from idea to final."

### [02:30 — 05:00] Templates

**[ON SCREEN]** Open Templates panel. Filter by mode. Click Fork on one.

**Narration:**
"Templates is the community library. Members share their best prompts and you can fork any of them as your starting point. Filter by mode or genre. When you find one you like, click Fork. The template loads into your active mode. From there it is yours. Edit it, generate from it, save your version. The original creator stays credited."

### [05:00 — 06:30] Submitting your own

**[ON SCREEN]** Click Submit Template. Fill in the form. Submit.

**Narration:**
"To give back, click Submit Template. Fill in the title, the description, the mode, the genre. Submit. Admins approve it and it goes live in the community library. This is how you build a reputation in the community. The more useful templates you submit, the more members fork your work."

### [06:30 — 08:30] Sharing a conversation

**[ON SCREEN]** Open Conversations. Click a conversation. Click Share. Enter an email.

**Narration:**
"Conversations is your full chat history. Click any conversation, then click Share. Enter the email of another community member. They get access immediately. They can read, respond, and contribute. You can revoke access any time from the Collaborators panel. This is how you co-write a song with someone in another city without sending files back and forth."

### [08:30 — 09:30] Collaboration etiquette

**[ON SCREEN]** Face cam.

**Narration:**
"Quick rules of the road. Always credit the person whose template you forked. If you share a conversation with a collaborator, give them edit access only if they are actually contributing. And if a template you submitted gets featured by an admin, that is on you to celebrate in the community. We win together."

### [09:30 — 10:00] Close

**Narration:**
"Next lesson, Release Prep and Clean Lyrics. We are getting your song ready to ship."
