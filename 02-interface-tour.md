# Lesson 02 — The Interface Tour

**Duration:** 8 minutes
**Outcome:** You can name every panel and button in the Suno Writer interface and know what each one does at a high level.
**Prerequisites:** Logged in to Suno Writer.

---

## Context (for the LMS module page)

Before you build anything, you need to know where everything lives. Suno Writer is laid out like a studio rack. Once you see the pattern, every mode feels familiar because they all share the same shell. This lesson is the map you will come back to whenever you forget where a button is.

## What You Will Learn
- The rack metaphor and why the layout works that way
- The 4 mode buttons and what each one is for
- The 3 view tabs that live inside every mode
- The sidebar (New Chat, Saved Works, Templates, Conversations, User Profile)
- The header icons (Flow Visualizer, Help tooltips, Clear buffer)
- The parameter panel (Complexity knob, Rating toggle, H-Pads)

---

## Step-by-Step Guide

1. Log in to Suno Writer.
2. Look at the top header and find the SUNO WRITER v2.0 label.
3. Look at the left sidebar. This is your library and account.
4. Look at the mode selector strip. Click each mode once and watch the main panel change.
5. Inside any mode, find the view tabs labeled CHAT, ARRANGE, and MOOD BOARD. Click each.
6. Find the Help toggle in the top right and turn it ON for the first week. Tooltips will appear when you hover over anything.
7. Find the parameter panel and locate the Complexity knob, the Rating toggle, and the H-Pads.
8. Open the User Profile in the sidebar and find the Default Mode setting.
9. Close everything and return to the dashboard.

## Screen Recording Script

### [00:00 — 00:20] Cold open

**[ON SCREEN]** Full Suno Writer dashboard. Cursor parked top-left.

**Narration:**
"Quick interface tour. By the end of these 8 minutes you will know the name of every button on the screen. Let's go zone by zone."

### [00:20 — 01:30] The rack metaphor

**[ON SCREEN]** Slow cursor sweep across the top header.

**Narration:**
"I designed this app like a studio rack because that is how producers already think. Top header, left sidebar, main panel, parameter strip. Every mode uses the same shell, so once you learn it once you know it for everything. Up here at the top you see SUNO WRITER v2.0. That is your home base."

### [01:30 — 02:30] The 4 mode buttons

**[ON SCREEN]** Slowly hover over each mode button. Tooltips fire.

**Narration:**
"Here are the four modes. Songwriting is for full songs with lyrics. Instrumental is for beats and remixes with no vocals. Sample Maker is for drumless chop-ready loops. Analysis is where you upload audio and the app breaks it down. Each one of these gets its own lesson, so do not worry about the details yet. Just know where they live."

### [02:30 — 03:30] The view tabs

**[ON SCREEN]** Click into Songwriting Mode. Highlight the CHAT, ARRANGE, MOOD BOARD tabs.

**Narration:**
"Inside every mode you have three view tabs. CHAT is where you talk to the engine and get your prompts. ARRANGE is where you lay out the song section by section. MOOD BOARD is where you get visual creative direction. You can flip between them at any time and your work follows you across views."

### [03:30 — 05:00] The sidebar

**[ON SCREEN]** Cursor walks down the left sidebar. Open and close each item.

**Narration:**
"Left side is your library and account. New Chat starts a fresh session. Saved Works is everything you have saved, filterable by mode. Templates is the community library. You can browse, fork, and submit your own. Conversations is your full chat history with sharing built in. And down at the bottom is your user profile where you set your default mode and your preferences."

### [05:00 — 06:00] Header icons

**[ON SCREEN]** Top right of the main panel. Hover each icon.

**Narration:**
"Top right of the main panel you have three icons. Flow Visualizer shows you how the engine is thinking, which is fun once you know what you are doing. Help tooltips, turn this ON for your first week. Hover over anything and a tooltip explains it. Clear buffer wipes the current chat without deleting it from your conversation history."

### [06:00 — 07:30] The parameter panel

**[ON SCREEN]** Open the parameter panel. Highlight Complexity knob, Rating toggle, H-Pads.

**Narration:**
"Now the fun part. The parameter panel is where you shape the output. The Complexity knob runs from 0 to 100. Zero is sparse, fifty is standard, one hundred is dense with key changes and beat switches. The Rating toggle sets explicit or clean lyrics. And these three H-Pads are your texture controls. Rhythm Builder, Harmonic Shift, and Texture/Depth. We will use all of these in real lessons coming up."

### [07:30 — 08:00] Close

**[ON SCREEN]** Pull back to full dashboard view.

**Narration:**
"That is the whole interface. Take a screenshot or grab the cheat sheet linked below. Next lesson we are going to log you in for real and set your defaults. See you there."
