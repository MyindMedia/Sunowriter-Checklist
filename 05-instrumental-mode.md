# Lesson 05 — Instrumental / Remix Mode

**Duration:** 12 minutes
**Outcome:** You generate beat-only Suno prompts with no vocals and shape them with the H-Pads.
**Prerequisites:** Lessons 02 and 04 complete.

---

## Context (for the LMS module page)

Instrumental Mode is for when you want a beat, a remix bed, or a backing track with no vocals at all. The app still gives you two prompts, but the Lyric Prompt becomes pure arrangement direction in brackets. No words. Just production cues, energy levels, and section-by-section instructions. This mode also unlocks the three H-Pads, which are your texture controls for shaping the feel without rewriting the prompt from scratch.

## What You Will Learn
- When to use Instrumental Mode instead of Songwriting Mode
- How the Lyric Prompt becomes arrangement-only with bracketed cues
- The three H-Pads: Rhythm Builder, Harmonic Shift, Texture/Depth
- How to read and edit the bracketed production direction
- How heavy short-tail 808s, dropouts, and beat switches show up in the output
- How to use the quick-prompt buttons to start fast

---

## Step-by-Step Guide

1. Click Instrumental Mode in the mode selector.
2. Confirm you are in the CHAT view.
3. Click one of the quick-prompt buttons or type your own idea. Example: "Dark cinematic trap with haunting synths and heavy 808s."
4. Hit send.
5. Read the Style Prompt. Notice how it focuses on instrumentation, tempo, and tone.
6. Read the Lyric Prompt. Notice how it contains ONLY bracketed cues like [intro: filtered pad, no drums], [drop: heavy 808s, hi-hat triplets], [beat switch: half time].
7. Click one of the H-Pads (Rhythm Builder, Harmonic Shift, or Texture/Depth) to shape the next response.
8. Send a follow-up: "Add a beat switch on the second drop and pull the energy back on the bridge."
9. Copy both prompts and paste into Suno.
10. Generate. Listen. Iterate.
11. Save when you love it.

**[ON SCREEN]** Instrumental Mode active.

**Narration:**
"Instrumental Mode. No vocals, just beats. By the end of these 12 minutes you will have generated a beat, shaped it with the H-Pads, and dropped it into Suno."

### [00:20 — 01:00] When to use it

**[ON SCREEN]** Face cam.

**Narration:**
"Use this mode when you want a beat to write over later, a remix bed for an artist you are working with, or a fully instrumental release. The structure rules from Songwriting Mode still apply. The hook still hits early. The bridge still modulates. You just get arrangement instead of lyrics."

### [01:00 — 02:30] The arrangement-only Lyric Prompt

**[ON SCREEN]** Pull up an example Instrumental Mode response. Highlight the Lyric Prompt code block. Zoom in on the brackets.

**Narration:**
"Look at this. The Lyric Prompt has zero words. It is all bracketed cues. Intro filtered pad no drums. Drop heavy 808s hi-hat triplets. Beat switch half time. This is how you tell Suno exactly what to do at every section without writing a single lyric. Suno reads brackets as production direction. We exploit that."

### [02:30 — 04:30] The H-Pads explained

**[ON SCREEN]** Highlight all three H-Pads. Hover each to show the tooltip. Click Rhythm Builder.

**Narration:**
"Three H-Pads. Rhythm Builder pushes the engine to focus on drum patterns, hi-hat rolls, and rhythmic complexity. Harmonic Shift adds key changes and chord modulations between sections. Texture/Depth adds atmospheric layers, reverb tails, and stereo width. Click one before you send a prompt and the engine biases the output that way. You can stack them too."

### [04:30 — 06:30] Quick-prompt button live build

**[ON SCREEN]** Click "Dark Cinematic Trap" quick button. Watch the prompt populate. Hit send.

**Narration:**
"Watch. I am going to click the Dark Cinematic Trap quick button. The prompt populates. Send. Response comes back. Style Prompt focused on cinematic strings, distorted 808s, sparse hats. Lyric Prompt is all brackets. Intro orchestral pad no drums. Drop distorted 808s, hi-hat triplets. Bridge filtered drop, vocal chops. Outro tape stop."

### [06:30 — 08:30] Shaping with an H-Pad

**[ON SCREEN]** Click Texture/Depth H-Pad. Type follow-up: "Add a beat switch on the second drop and pull the energy back on the bridge."

**Narration:**
"Now I want more atmosphere. Click Texture/Depth. Now I am going to type, add a beat switch on the second drop and pull the energy back on the bridge. Send. The new response keeps the same DNA but adds reverb tails, ambient pads, and the energy curve I asked for."

### [08:30 — 10:30] Paste and generate

**[ON SCREEN]** Switch to Suno. Paste Style. Paste Lyric. Hit Create.

**Narration:**
"Switch to Suno. Paste the Style Prompt. Paste the Lyric Prompt. Create. Suno reads the brackets as section instructions and gives you back a beat that actually has the structure you wrote, not just a vibe."

### [10:30 — 11:30] Stacking H-Pads

**[ON SCREEN]** Back in Suno Writer. Click Rhythm Builder ON top of Texture/Depth. Send another follow-up.

**Narration:**
"You can stack H-Pads too. Now I am layering Rhythm Builder on top of Texture/Depth. Same beat, more rhythmic complexity, more atmosphere. This is how you evolve a beat across iterations without losing the original vibe."

### [11:30 — 12:00] Save and close

**[ON SCREEN]** Click Save. Name the work.

**Narration:**
"Save it. Tag it. Done. Homework, build one beat, swap one H-Pad, post both versions in the community. Next lesson, we go drumless and chop-ready."
