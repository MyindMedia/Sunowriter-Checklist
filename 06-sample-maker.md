# Lesson 06 — Sample Maker Mode

**Duration:** 10 minutes
**Outcome:** You generate a drumless, chop-ready loop you can drag into your DAW and sample.
**Prerequisites:** Lessons 04 and 05 complete.

---

## Context (for the LMS module page)

Sample Maker Mode is the secret weapon. It generates drumless songs and loops designed to be chopped, flipped, and sampled the way producers have been sampling soul, jazz, and orchestral records for decades. No drums, ever. The hook always comes first so you can chop it immediately. Use it to create your own sample library that nobody else has access to.

## What You Will Learn
- Why drumless and chop-ready matters
- The genres this mode is built for
- The "no drums, organic percussion only" rule
- Why the hook comes first
- How to feed the output back into Instrumental Mode for a full beat

---

## Step-by-Step Guide

1. Click Sample Maker Mode.
2. Type your idea. Example: "Motown soul loop, female vocal lead, warm tape compression, hopeful and bittersweet."
3. Hit send.
4. Read the Style Prompt. Notice it specifies no drums and only organic percussion (tambourine, congas, shakers).
5. Read the Lyric Prompt. The hook always appears first.
6. Copy both into Suno and generate.
7. Download the Suno result.
8. Drag the file into your DAW.
9. Chop the hook section first.
10. (Bonus) Take the same vibe back to Instrumental Mode and generate a beat that sits underneath your chop.

### [00:30 — 01:30] Why drumless matters

**[ON SCREEN]** Face cam.

**Narration:**
"Producers have been sampling soul, jazz, and orchestral records for fifty years. The reason it works is because the source material has melody, harmony, and emotion without locked-in drums. You drop your own drums underneath. That is the entire art form. Sample Maker Mode generates source material that is built exactly for that workflow."

### [01:30 — 02:30] The rules

**[ON SCREEN]** Pull up a Sample Maker output. Highlight the no-drums rule and the organic percussion line.

**Narration:**
"Three hard rules. No drums ever. Only organic percussion like tambourine, congas, or shakers. And the hook always comes first so you can chop it immediately when you drop it in your DAW. These rules are baked into the system prompt. You cannot break them by accident."

### [02:30 — 03:30] The genres it shines in

**[ON SCREEN]** Show a list overlay of genre options.

**Narration:**
"This mode lives in Motown soul, folk, world music, jazz, orchestral phrases, and vocal snippets. If it sounds like something you would dig out of a crate, it works here. If you want trap or EDM, use Instrumental Mode instead."

### [03:30 — 06:00] Live build

**[ON SCREEN]** Type: "Motown soul loop, female vocal lead, warm tape compression, hopeful and bittersweet." Send. Read response.

**Narration:**
"Let me build one. Motown soul loop, female vocal lead, warm tape compression, hopeful and bittersweet. Send. Look at the Style Prompt. Warm Rhodes, walking bass, tambourine and shakers, no drum kit. Look at the Lyric Prompt. The hook lands in the first eight bars. That is so I can chop it the second I drag the file into my DAW."

### [06:00 — 07:30] Generate and download

**[ON SCREEN]** Switch to Suno. Paste both prompts. Generate. Download the result.

**Narration:**
"Paste into Suno. Generate. Download. Now I have a sample I generated myself, that no other producer has access to, that I own."

### [07:30 — 09:00] Chop in DAW

**[ON SCREEN]** Drag the audio file into Ableton (or your DAW of choice). Slice the hook.

**Narration:**
"Drag the file into Ableton. Slice the hook. This is just like chopping any other sample, except this one was generated for you, on demand, in your style. You can do this for every project, every release."

### [09:00 — 09:45] Bonus: feed it back

**[ON SCREEN]** Switch back to Suno Writer, open Instrumental Mode, type a follow-up referencing the same vibe.

**Narration:**
"Bonus move. Take the same vibe back to Instrumental Mode and generate a beat that sits underneath your chop. Now you have a full track built around a sample you made yourself. That is the whole loop closed."

### [09:45 — 10:00] Close

**Narration:**
"Homework, generate one Sample Maker loop, chop it in your DAW, post the chop in the community. Next lesson we get analytical."
